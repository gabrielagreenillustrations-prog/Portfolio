# Redesigning n1co's Loyalty Program (co1ns)
## From inflated rewards to a sustainable gamified system

---

## Overview

n1co's loyalty program, co1ns, rewarded users through transactions and challenges. However, users were not engaging or redeeming rewards — the program existed but wasn't working.

---

## Problem

The core issues pointed to a broken reward economy:

- **Currency inflation:** co1ns had lost perceived value
- **Rewards felt unreachable:** the gap between earning and redeeming was too large
- **Progress tied only to spending:** no alternative paths to advancement
- **Low awareness:** users didn't understand how the system worked or what they could get from it

---

## Strategy

Shift the mental model from a single-currency system to a dual-track structure:

**Before:** co1ns = everything
**After:**
- **co1ns** → rewards (purchasing power)
- **Activity points** → progression (rank advancement)

This separation gave the economy two levers to tune independently, and gave users two distinct motivations to engage.

---

## Key Decisions

- Introduce activity points as a separate progression currency
- Redesign levels into ranks with visible milestones
- Add evaluation cycles to create natural engagement loops
- Rename "Shop" → "Rewards" to set clearer expectations
- Rebalance the economy to make redemption feel attainable

---

## Gamification

Three mechanics layered on top of the new economy:

- **Roulette:** chance-based reward moments
- **Streaks:** consistency incentives
- **Achievements:** milestone recognition

---

## My Role

**UX/UI Designer** at n1co technologies (2022–2023).

Worked on the end-to-end redesign of the loyalty experience, including the rank progression system, cashback boosts, and activity point architecture.

---

## Results

- +45% increase in user engagement
- +60% improvement in progression rates
- +35% increase in reward redemption
- −40% reduction in user complaints about the program

---

## Learnings

- Loyalty programs fail when earning feels easy but redeeming feels impossible — fixing that gap is the core design challenge.
- Separating progression from purchasing power gave the system the flexibility to reward different behaviors without devaluing the currency.
- Gamification only works when the underlying economy is sound. Mechanics on top of a broken system just add noise.
